The Recovery Stack

Recovery is not an event.
It is a stack of rhythms.

Three layers, operating at three timescales. Most executives only recover when they collapse. The ones who sustain output for decades have built the stack deliberately — daily, weekly, quarterly — as infrastructure, not as something they do when there's time.

03

Quarterly Reset

One real vacation per quarter. One reflection day. One annual sabbatical. The hardest layer to install — the highest-leverage practice in the stack.

02

Weekly Rhythm

One full work-free day. One untouched evening. One block of physical effort. One unstructured hour. Replenishes what daily recovery cannot.

01

Daily Recovery

One outdoor walk. One real meal. One transition ritual. One screen-free hour. Small. Non-negotiable. Compounds enormously over a year.

The Wellness Diagnostic

Where most executives quietly compromise themselves

Five patterns show up consistently in burnt-out executives. Most recognize at least two before naming any of them out loud.

01

Recovery has stopped working

A weekend used to refresh. A vacation used to reset. When neither does, the system has been depleted longer than you realize.

02

Decision velocity has collapsed

What used to take 20 minutes now takes 3 days. The problem hasn't changed. The cognitive bandwidth has narrowed.

03

Sleep architecture is broken

Hours in bed look fine. Deep sleep is an hour short. REM is cut at the alarm. You can't out-coffee a sleep deficit.

04

Emotional range has narrowed

Wins don't feel like wins. Setbacks feel personally devastating. The system is protecting itself by reducing emotional load.

05

Quietly skipping the layers

Sleep, training, meals, recovery — on the calendar but increasingly skipped when "things get busy." The deficit accumulates silently.
